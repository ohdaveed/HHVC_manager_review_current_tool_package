## PESTS, VERMIN, ANIMALS

### Rodents (Sec 581(b)(13))

**Purpose:** Rodents can spread disease, contaminate food, and damage structures.

**Signs of a Problem:** Droppings; gnaw marks on food packaging; scratching sounds in walls or ceilings; burrows near foundation or yard; grease marks along walls.

**What to Do Now:** Store all food in metal, glass, or hard plastic containers; remove accessible food and trash; eliminate standing water sources; remove clutter and storage from floors; report visible holes or openings.

**Responsibilities**

- **Tenant:** Maintain sanitation; store food properly; report activity immediately; prepare unit for inspection/treatment
- **Property Owner:** Seal all entry points larger than 1/4 inch; repair structural defects; provide pest control services; eliminate harborage conditions

**Call to Action:** Already reported this and it wasn't fixed? → Report a residential building concern

**What Happens Next:** An inspector may evaluate the property. Violations must be corrected and may require follow-up inspection.

**Learn More:** Rodents require food, water, and shelter. Eliminating all three is required for control.

### Cockroaches (Sec 581(b)(8))

**Signs:** Small dark droppings; live insects, especially at night; egg casings; grease marks.

**What to Do:** Clean food debris daily; seal food containers; fix leaks; reduce clutter; do not rely on sprays alone.

**Responsibilities:** Tenant: sanitation, reporting. Owner: pest control, structural repair.

### Bed Bugs (Sec 581(b)(8))

**Signs:** Bites in clusters; blood spots on bedding; dark spotting on mattress seams.

**What to Do:** Report immediately; do not move items between rooms; follow preparation instructions.

**Responsibilities:** Tenant: preparation, reporting. Owner: treatment coordination.

### Flies (Sec 581(b)(8))

**Signs:** Large numbers of flies; maggots in garbage; fly spotting on surfaces.

**What to Do:** Remove waste promptly; clean garbage areas; eliminate breeding material.

### Mosquitoes (Sec 581(b)(8))

**Signs:** Standing water; mosquito activity; larvae in containers.

**What to Do:** Dump standing water weekly; remove or cover containers; clean gutters and drains.

### Pigeons (Sec 581(b)(7))

**Signs:** Accumulated droppings; nesting activity; roosting areas.

**What to Do:** Remove attractants; clean droppings safely; install exclusion measures.

### Poison Oak (Sec 581(b)(11))

**Signs:** Identifiable toxic plants; overgrowth in yard or lot.

**What to Do:** Avoid contact; remove safely or hire professional.

## SANITATION

### Garbage / Refuse / Debris (Sec 581(b)(1))

**Signs:** Accumulated trash; food waste buildup; odors.

**What to Do:** Remove waste regularly; use proper containers; clean surrounding areas.

### Human / Animal Waste (Sec 581(b)(1)(5))

**Signs:** Visible feces; strong odor; contamination.

**What to Do:** Remove immediately; clean and disinfect affected areas; use proper PPE if needed.

### Overgrown Vegetation (Sec 581(b)(2))

**Signs:** Dense plant growth; inaccessible yard areas; hidden structures.

**What to Do:** Cut vegetation; remove debris; maintain visibility and access.

---

## STRUCTURAL / INTERIOR CONDITIONS

### Mold Growth (Sec 581(b)(6))

**Signs:** Visible mold; musty odor; moisture or condensation.

**What to Do:** Report leaks; improve ventilation; clean small areas after fixing moisture source.

### Excessive Materials (Sec 581(b)(18))

**Signs:** Cluttered living areas; blocked access; inability to inspect.

**What to Do:** Remove excess storage; elevate remaining items; clear pathways and access.

---

## GARBAGE AREA

### Uncontainerized Garbage (Sec 581(b)(1))

**Signs:** Loose trash; exposed food waste; pest activity.

**What to Do:** Place waste in proper containers; keep lids closed; prevent overflow.
