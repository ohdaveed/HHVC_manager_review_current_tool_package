Audience callouts: residential hotel vs. regular hotel

No new pages or hub — using the audience field that already exists on every page (see report-cockroaches.js) plus a short callout box where the distinction actually changes what someone should do.

Example: Report cockroaches — audience list + callout

Who this is for

  • A tenant affected by cockroaches
  • A friend, family member, advocate, or helper reporting for a tenant
  • A person reporting cockroaches in an SRO kitchen, bathroom, or shared area
  • An employee reporting cockroaches at work
  • A resident of a residential hotel (SRO)
Staying at a hotel? HHVC also inspects regular hotels, not just residential hotels (SROs) and shelters. Report the problem here the same way.
Example: Records hub — tool selection callout

Which tool do I use?

• Apartment or residential hotel (SRO): use "Find complaints & inspection records"

• Residential Hotel Program facility specifically, or a shelter: use "Find residential hotel & shelter records"

Why this fits better than a new hub

  • Residential hotel guidance isn't a separate journey — someone in an SRO reports a cockroach problem the exact same way a regular tenant does, they just need to see themselves in the audience list
  • Reuses a field this tool already tracks per-page (audience), so it's a content edit, not a new component or graph node
