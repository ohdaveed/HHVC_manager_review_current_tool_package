## 1. Short Summary

Report mold or moisture caused by humidity, condensation, or poor ventilation.

---

## 2. Who this page is for

This page can help if you rent or live in a San Francisco home and need to report mold or moisture caused by humidity, condensation, or poor ventilation.

This page can also help property owners or managers understand when a mold or humidity concern may be reported through 311.

---

## 3. Before you report

If you rent your home, tell your property owner or manager about the problem in writing first. Give them **at least 72 hours** to respond or start fixing the problem.

If the problem continues, the owner or manager does not respond, or there is an immediate health or safety concern, report the issue through 311.

---

## 4. Where does your water leak belong? (Agency Routing)

Water problems in San Francisco are split between two City agencies depending on the physical source of the moisture. To get help quickly, make sure you choose the right path.

### Department of Building Inspection (DBI)

Contact DBI if the moisture is caused by building infrastructure failures or structural water intrusion under the San Francisco Housing Code. This includes:

- Roof leaks (water penetrating ceilings or attics) (Housing Code SEC. 703)
- Leaking window frames (failing exterior caulking or flashing) (Housing Code SEC. 703)
- Plumbing leaks (burst pipes, dripping pipes inside walls) (Housing Code SEC. 703)
- Malfunctioning or leaking water heaters (Housing Code SEC. 703)
- Broken major appliances provided by the landlord (dishwashers, washing machines)

### Healthy Housing and Vector Control (HHVC / SFDPH)

Report to 311 for HHVC review if the mold or moisture is caused by condensation, humidity, or mechanical ventilation failures. This includes:

- Active mold on walls or ceilings caused by high humidity
- Exhaust fan failures (inoperable bathroom or kitchen fans)
- Persistent condensation ("sweat") on windows or walls due to poor air circulation

---

## 5. What you can report to HHVC

You can report moisture or mold conditions that fall under HHVC review, such as:

- Active visible mold on structural surfaces (walls or ceilings) that totals **at least 10 square feet** inside a home, apartment, or residential building.
- Inoperable mechanical exhaust fans in kitchens or bathrooms that prevent moisture from being expelled.
- For SROs or residential hotels: active mold, moisture, or ventilation issues affecting private rooms or shared areas (such as communal kitchens, communal bathrooms, hallways, or garbage rooms).

### What you cannot report:

- **No Personal Property Jurisdiction:** HHVC does not have legal authority over personal belongings. You cannot report mold found on furniture, clothes, mattresses, bedding, carpets, or personal household items. It is the resident's responsibility to clean or discard personal items.

---

## 6. Report through 311

Report this issue through 311.DPH will investigate valid housing complaints and can require property owners to correct violations.

[**Report Mold or Moisture Online**](https://www.sf311.org)
_You can also report by calling 311 inside San Francisco or (415) 701-2311 from outside the city._

---

## 7. What to include in your report

To help inspectors review your concern, please provide:

- The exact address and apartment or room number
- Where the mold or moisture is located (for example: bathroom ceiling, SRO communal kitchen)
- How long the problem has been happening
- Whether you notified the property owner or manager in writing, and if they responded
- Descriptive details of the problem (such as "the bathroom fan has been broken for two weeks and mold is spreading on the ceiling")

_Note on Photos:_ Do **not** require photos as a standard instruction. Photos are not required to submit a report and may not be visible to inspectors through the 311 intake routing system.

---

## 8. What happens after you report

After a report is submitted, HHVC may review the complaint to verify it involves a public health concern.

- **With Contact Info:** If you provide your name and phone number, an inspector may contact you to ask follow-up questions, discuss the issue, or schedule a physical inspection visit.
- **Without Contact Info:** If no contact information is provided, an inspection may still occur without notice when appropriate.
- **Enforcement:** If an inspection identifies violations of the San Francisco Health Code, the inspector will issue a Notice of Violation requiring the property owner to correct the conditions within a specific compliance period. Reinspection fees or citations may apply if the conditions are not corrected.
- **No Guarantees:** The City does not promise exact contact times, guaranteed inspection dates, or specific enforcement timelines.
