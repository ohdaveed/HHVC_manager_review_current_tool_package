## NOTICE

**Vegetation Overgrowth is a violation of San Francisco Health Code Sec. 581**

Don't give shelter to rats, mice, raccoons and mosquitoes, which can pass diseases to people.

## Do I have Vegetation Overgrowth on my property?

- Can you walk through all areas of your yard?
- Can you see the top of your fence, or is it covered with ivy?
- Is the groundcover knee high or higher? Do you have a pile of dead branches?
- Do trees or shrubs contact the walls and roof of your house or extend into adjacent sidewalks?

## I have received a Notice of Violation — What should I do?

- Contact the investigator named on the notice. You save money if fewer inspections are made.
- If you fail to correct the violation(s) you will be cited to appear at a public hearing.
- Make a personal survey of the property by looking for the conditions described above.
- Consult with a gardener if possible to avoid problems like erosion on slopes.
- If hiring someone to do the work, get estimates and references.
- Be sure to dispose lawfully of all rubbish that is removed. Keep dump receipts.
- Plan for regular maintenance to avoid future problems.

## Tips for Pruning and thinning trees and shrubs

- Use personal protective equipment (PPE) such as gloves and safety glasses.
- Pruning targets limbs touching buildings, dead branches, crossing branches, suckers, water sprouts.
- Thinning shrubs allows air and light to reach all areas. Eliminates shelter for rodents and mosquitoes.

## Tips for Controlling Weeds

Maintain groundcover below 24" high. Weed control is accomplished through a combination of hand weeding, use of mulch, and using herbicides if necessary. Mulch is a protective layer placed over the soil, usually a material such as straw or woodchips, but gravel and other materials may be used. Mulch controls weeds by blocking the sunlight, retains water, adds nutrients to the soil, repels insects, and controls soil erosion. Mulching can reduce landscape maintenance costs. When using mulch for weed control, maintain the layer at two inch to three inches in depth.
