### **8.1 Purpose**

This chapter establishes the public-facing content standards for all Healthy Housing and Vector Control (HHVC) services on SF.gov. It defines the legal, operational, and technical boundaries governing tenant complaints, property inspections, code enforcement timelines, program fees, and the integration of secure third-party web forms. By codifying these standards, HHVC ensures that complex administrative processes are translated into plain, actionable, and legally citable instructions.

---

### **8.2 Tenant Complaint and Reporting Pathway**

Public-facing pages designed for reporting building concerns must follow a strict sequential pathway to ensure residents exhaust direct communication channels before triggering formal municipal investigations.

#### **8.2.1 Mandatory Written Landlord Notification**

All pest, mold, and sanitation reporting pages must prominently display a blue Callout block instructing tenants to notify their landlord or building manager in writing first. HHVC uses **three distinct timelines** — do not merge them into one rule:

- **Tenant 72-hour notice before 311 (program standard):** Tenants should give the property owner or manager **written notice** and, when possible, wait **72 hours (3 days)** before filing with 311. Urgent health or safety dangers may be reported immediately. This is a program routing standard documented in the Compliance Standards tracker — not a single Article 11A citation for all pests.
- **Owner 72-hour investigation (Director's Rules):** After a tenant reports pest activity, the owner or manager must investigate within **72 hours** (legacy Director's Rules §V.2; restore in v4.0 §V when published).
- **Bed bug PCO response (Article 11A):** For bed bugs, licensed professionals must respond within **two working days** — a separate clock from the tenant notice and owner investigation rules. Adjacent-unit inspection duties also apply under Director's Rules §VI.6.

#### **8.2.2 Clear Actionable Escalation to 311**

If the landlord fails to act within the legal notice windows, the page must provide a direct, single call-to-action (CTA) button to submit a report to 311:

- **CTA Labeling:** The button must use active, verb-first language:
  - _Approved:_ `Report a building concern`
  - _Prohibited:_ `Click here`, `Submit`, or `Get started`
- **311 Integration:** Reporting pages must link directly to the responsive SF311 online reporting form.

#### **8.2.3 Tenant Responsibilities**

Tenants are also responsible for keeping their homes free of pests under Article 11 and the Director's Rules. Public-facing pages must present tenant duties in clear, plain language:

- **Routine housekeeping:** Tenants must keep living areas reasonably clean and orderly, and keep garbage, pet food, and other attractive items properly stored.
- **Wildlife prohibitions:** Tenants must not put food out for wildlife or pigeons. This is both a sanitation standard and a vector prevention rule.
- **Pre-treatment cooperation:** Tenants must allow reasonable access for pest control professionals, inspectors, and maintenance workers when scheduled, and follow preparation instructions before treatment.
- **Clutter reduction:** Clutter gives pests places to hide and makes inspections and treatments less effective.

### **8.3 DPH/HHVC vs. DBI Jurisdictional Split**

To prevent misrouted complaints and protect resident response times, all healthy housing pages must clearly separate biological hazards from structural, mechanical, and utility failures.

#### **8.3.1 HHVC Biological and Sanitation Scope**

The SFDPH Healthy Housing and Vector Control program has exclusive jurisdiction over:

1.  **Biological Vectors:** Active infestations of rats, mice, bed bugs, cockroaches, flies, fleas, wasps, raccoons, and pigeons.
2.  **Environmental Health Hazards:** Mold growth, severe dampness, lead paint exposure, and garbage accumulation on private property.
3.  **Special Programs:** SRO hotel hygiene, communal kitchen standards, and tourist hotel sanitation.

4.  **Standing Water and Mosquito Breeding:** Property owners and managers must eliminate all standing water sources that support mosquito breeding. Standing water also attracts wildlife, rodents, and other unwanted pests.5.  **Landscape Management:** When rodent activity is identified, owners must thin out dense trees, vines, shrubs, and groundcover that can hide nests, burrows, runways, and drains.6.  **Vector Exclusion:** Buildings must be constructed and maintained to prevent the entry of vectors or pests. Gaps greater than **1/4 inch** around exterior doors and windows must be repaired; openings in exterior walls, particularly around pipes or conduits, must be sealed with hardware cloth, copper mesh, caulk, sheet metal, concrete, or mortar.7.  **Pigeon Roosting and Pigeon-Proofing:** Pigeon waste, feathers, and nesting materials are a source of mites, lice, allergens, bacteria, fungal spores, and other organisms that can cause human disease. Accumulation of these materials constitutes a health nuisance. In San Francisco, it is not legal to poison, trap, or shoot pigeons. Pigeon-proofing means pigeons are discouraged and prevented from roosting and creating a health nuisance by means of design, such as sloping sills, or by means of a net, screen, or other deterrent such as a timed sprinkler.8.  **Pet Waste and Animal Attractants:** Animal waste from pets attracts flies and rodents and other disease vectors, is a source of nuisance odors, and any accumulation is a violation of the Health Code. Tenants must not put food out for wildlife or pigeons.
#### **8.3.2 DBI Structural and Utility Scope**

All structural, mechanical, plumbing, electrical, and life-safety issues fall under the exclusive jurisdiction of the **Department of Building Inspection (DBI)**. Healthy housing pages must explicitly state that the following issues are out of HHVC scope and provide a direct link to DBI's reporting forms:

- **Plumbing Failures:** Lack of hot or cold running water, low water pressure, and major leaks (Housing Code SEC. 701, 703).
- **Mechanical Failures:** Non-functional heating systems during winter cycles (Housing Code SEC. 701).
- **Structural Safety:** Structural dry rot, collapsing ceilings, sagging floors, broken windows, and loose handrails (Housing Code SEC. 703).
- **Egress Hazards:** Blocked fire escapes, locked exit doors, and non-functional emergency lighting (Housing Code SEC. 810).
- **Vertical Transit:** Out-of-service elevators in buildings with seven or more stories (Housing Code SEC. 713/808).

### **8.4 Notice of Violation (NOV) Timelines**

Public-facing pages explaining the inspection process must outline clear compliance windows so property owners understand their legal obligations and potential penalties:

1.  **Urgent Hazards (48 to 72 Hours):** Severe sewage backups, active lead exposure in households with young children, or lack of heating in SRO hotels require immediate correction within **48 to 72 hours**.
2.  **General Violations (30 Days):** Standard vector infestations, mild mold, and minor sanitation issues must be corrected within **30 calendar days** of the NOV date.
3.  **Extension Requests:** Public content must explain that extensions are possible only if the property owner contacts the assigned inspector _before_ the compliance deadline and provides a certified, written contract with a licensed Pest Control Operator (PCO) or general contractor.

### **8.5 Program Fees and Payment Standards**

To maintain public credibility and legal compliance, all financial references must reflect the **most recently published certified rate schedule** — currently the SFDPH Environmental Health Branch fee schedule effective **7/1/26–6/30/27** (see `docs/source/hhvc-policy/2026-07-07-fy27-website-fees.md`). Public fee pages must clearly separate annual billing from penalty fees:

#### **8.5.1 Annual Apartment and SRO Program Fees**

The annual healthy housing fee is billed progressively based on the total unit count of the residential structure. Karl Transaction and Information pages do not support a table block — **Report** is the only content type with native tables (see Chapter 4 §4.2). Fee tiers belong on a linked **Resource Collection** or **Report** document, not a hardcoded table on a Transaction page. For a live SF.gov visual example of where tables belong, see the Rent Board **Current Rates** page pattern in [`karl-content-type-field-reference.md`](../docs/source/hhvc-policy/karl-content-type-field-reference.md) (Report screenshot appendix).

| Number of Rental Units                             | Annual Program Fee |
| :------------------------------------------------- | :----------------: |
| **3 Units** (Exempt if one unit is owner-occupied) |    **$103.00**     |
| **4 to 6 Units**                                   |    **$129.00**     |
| **7 to 10 Units**                                  |    **$174.00**     |
| **11 to 15 Units**                                 |    **$350.00**     |
| **16 to 20 Units**                                 |    **$485.00**     |
| **21 to 30 Units**                                 |    **$688.00**     |
| **Over 30 Units**                                  |    **$808.00**     |

_Note: SRO and Hotel Program fees bill on a separate April-to-March license cycle, ranging from **$554.00** (under 20 units) up to **$1,976.00** (structures with more than 175 units)._

#### **8.5.2 Hourly Reinspection Fees**

Reinspection fees are designed to recover administrative costs when property owners fail to correct violations by the designated NOV deadline. Public content must explicitly warn landlords of these rates:

- **Start of Fees:** Reinspection fees are charged starting on the **third visit** (the second reinspection following the initial NOV check).
- **Hourly Inspector Rate:** Billed at **$256.00 per hour** (additional half-hour increments: **$128.00**).
- **Hourly Technician Rate:** Billed at **$234.00 per hour** (additional half-hour increments: **$115.00**).

#### **8.5.3 Late Penalties and Property Liens**

Property owners must be warned of the progressive financial penalties for unpaid annual fees or reinspection assessments:

- **30-Day Penalty:** A progressive late fee of **$10.00** is assessed after 30 days of delinquency.
- **60-Day Penalty:** An escalated penalty of **$30.00** is assessed after 60 days.
- **Lien Recordation:** If fees remain unpaid, DPH is legally authorized to record a property tax lien against the parcel. Liens accrue a monthly compounded interest rate of **1.5%** until paid in full.

### **8.6 External Web Forms and Fillout.com Integration Standards**

To streamline non-complaint service requests (such as public workshop registrations, mosquito education presentations, or program sign-ups), HHVC is authorized to use secure external web forms powered by **Fillout.com**. All third-party forms integrated into public SF.gov pages must strictly comply with the following structural, security, and accessibility standards:

#### **8.6.1 Karl Page-Type Selection & URL Scheme**

- **Transaction Page Blueprint:** Any page hosting a Fillout service request must be built as a Karl **Transaction page**. Transaction pages are reserved for single, direct user exchanges with the City.
- **No Multi-Tasking:** The page must focus exclusively on driving the user toward form submission. Do not combine educational vector biology or historical background on the transaction page; use the right-hand Related panel to link to separate Information pages.
- **URL slug:** Transaction pages live directly after the domain root (e.g., `sf.gov/request-mosquito-education`).

#### **8.6.2 The Hosted Link Standard (Safest CTA Approach)**

To maintain complete platform compatibility and ensure mobile responsiveness, HHVC enforces a **Hosted Link** standard:

- **CTA Button Link:** Editors must use a standard Karl **Button component** placed prominently at the top of the "What to do" section.
- **Destination URL:** The button must link directly to the hosted Fillout form URL (e.g., `https://forms.fillout.com/t/<form-id>`).
- **No Script or Iframe Embeds:** Because custom inline scripts and iframe embeds frequently break Karl's responsive stylesheets and screen-reader paths, **editors are strictly prohibited from pasting iframe or javascript embed codes into Karl text fields**. Any inline form embed requires direct technical review and implementation by the SF.gov Digital Services technical team.
- **Descriptive Screen Reader Labels:** The Button component must include a clear, descriptive screen reader label that outlines the action (e.g., "Request a mosquito education workshop form on Fillout.com") to maintain WCAG 2.1 Level AA conformance.

#### **8.6.3 Security, Privacy, and Due Diligence Controls**

While Fillout.com maintains SOC 2 Type 2 compliance and encrypts data both in transit and at rest, HHVC editors must implement strict data-minimization controls:

1.  **Do Not Collect by Default:** Third-party forms must **never** collect highly sensitive or restricted public records, including:
    - Medical information or history (HIPAA risk).
    - Immigration or citizenship status.
    - Social Security Numbers (SSNs) or financial passwords.
    - Exact home addresses or photos unless operationally necessary for site visits.
2.  **Legal Disclaimer Requirement:** Staged pages must feature a standard privacy disclaimer informing users that the form is processed by a third-party vendor and outlining data-retention schedules. DPH privacy and vendor review must be completed prior to form launch.

#### **8.6.4 Source Tracking and Analytics Configuration**

To measure the reach of educational campaigns, all Fillout.com forms must be configured with hidden URL tracking parameters:

- **Hidden Fields:** The back end of the Fillout form must contain the following hidden tracking fields:
  - `source_page` (e.g., `sf-gov-mosquito-education-request`)
  - `program` (set to `hhvc`)
  - `request_category` (e.g., `mosquito_education`)
  - `page_type` (set to `transaction`)
  - `utm_source` / `utm_campaign` (inherited automatically from SF.gov URLs)

#### **8.6.5 Conversational Form Design and Branching Logic**

To prevent "form fatigue" and ensure high mobile completion rates, Fillout forms must utilize conditional branching logic:

- **Branching Rules:**
  - _If "Presentation" selected:_ Show fields for preferred dates, expected audience size, and specific topic needs.
  - _If "Tabling/Event" selected:_ Show fields for event date, physical location, and expected community attendance.
  - _If "Printed Materials" selected:_ Show fields for material type, language preference, and pickup/delivery options.
- **Plain-Language Guidance:** Form fields must use direct, active questions in sentence case (e.g., "What date would you like us to visit?").

### **8.7 Plain-Language Article 11 and 11A Code Summaries**

Public-facing pages explaining biological hazards must translate complex legal codes into simple, citable summaries. Editors must **never** copy-paste raw, bureaucratic municipal code blocks onto general service pages.

#### **8.7.1 Standard Plain-Language Summaries**

- **Health Code Article 11 (Vermin Nuisances):** Public pages must summarize this code in clear terms: _“Under San Francisco Health Code Article 11, property owners are legally required to keep their buildings clean, watertight, and completely free of rodent and insect infestations”_.
- **Health Code Article 11A (Bed Bug Abatement):** Summarize this code as: _“Under San Francisco Health Code Article 11A, landlords must respond to bed bug reports promptly, ensure licensed professionals treat active infestations within two working days, and inspect adjacent units as required.”_ Do not use the 72-hour tenant-notice clock as the bed bug PCO deadline.

#### **8.7.2 Direct Municipal Citations**

Every page referencing legal obligations must include a direct, descriptive hyperlink to the official municipal code publisher:

- _Approved Link:_ `View the full text of [Health Code Article 11](url) on the municipal code website.`
- _Prohibited Link:_ `Click here to read the law.`
