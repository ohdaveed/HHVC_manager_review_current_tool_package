## Why this matters (Key Takeaway)

The San Francisco Department of Public Health (SFDPH) enforces Health Code Article 11 to keep residential buildings safe, clean, and free of pests. Knowing what we can inspect helps you choose the correct reporting path and get your housing concern fixed faster.

---

## What HHVC can inspect

Our inspectors review housing health, sanitation, and pest conditions at residential properties in San Francisco. We investigate active problems and enforce corrective rules.

### 1. Pests and Vector Concerns

We inspect for pests that can spread disease, damage property, or cause severe health stress:

- **Rats or mice:** We look for active nesting, droppings, and holes or cracks (entry points) in walls or foundations that let rodents inside.
- **Cockroaches:** We inspect active infestations inside homes, kitchens, bathrooms, basements, utility rooms, and shared areas.
- **Bed bugs:** We inspect active infestations in rental housing. Owners must investigate reports within 72 hours and direct a licensed Pest Control Operator (PCO) to inspect all adjacent units (above, below, and side-to-side).
- **Flies:** We inspect active fly infestations and enforce fly control requirements under the San Francisco Health Code. This includes house flies and blow flies breeding in garbage or organic waste.
- **Ground wasps:** We inspect suspected ground wasp nests to verify safety hazards.
- **Pigeons:** We inspect pigeon roosting, nesting, or droppings that create unsanitary conditions or odors near doors, windows, vents, or shared areas.
- **Mites:** We review housing conditions linked to tropical rat mites, which often leave rodent nests and bite people after a rat infestation.

### 2. Sanitation and Housing Conditions

We inspect conditions that attract pests or affect indoor health:

- **Garbage and clutter:** Accumulations of trash, rotting food, or cardboard debris inside or outside a building that can attract or breed pests (Health Code Sec. 581(b)(1)).
- **Animal waste:** Accumulated dog or cat waste in yards, patios, or common building areas.
- **Overgrown vegetation:** Thick weeds, vines, or overgrown brush close to walls and foundations that shelter pests or block safe access to building exits (Health Code Sec. 581(b)(2)).
- **Indoor moisture and humidity-related mold:** We inspect visible mold growing on walls or ceilings caused by high humidity, poor mechanical ventilation (like broken kitchen or bathroom exhaust fans), or condensation. Action is taken under Health Code Sec. 581(b)(6) when visible mold totals at least 10 square feet.

## What we cannot inspect (Correct Routing)

Some housing problems are handled by other City departments. To get help quickly, check who handles your issue before reporting:

### 1. Structural water leaks, heating, and building damage (DBI)

The Department of Building Inspection (DBI) handles structural building failures under the San Francisco Housing Code. **Do not report structural issues to HHVC.** Contact DBI for:

- Leaking roof frames, window seals, or water coming through the ceiling (Housing Code SEC. 703)
- Plumbing pipe breaks, sewage backups, or dripping pipes inside walls (Housing Code SEC. 703)
- Broken or leaking water heaters (Housing Code SEC. 703)
- Malfunctioning heating systems or lack of heat (Housing Code SEC. 701)
- Elevator breakdowns (Housing Code SEC. 713 & 808) and obstructed fire escapes or exits (Housing Code SEC. 810 & 803)

### 2. Personal property and belongings

HHVC only has jurisdiction over the building's structure (walls, ceilings, common areas). **We cannot inspect or enforce repairs on personal belongings.** This means we cannot assist with:

- Mold growing on your personal furniture, mattress, clothing, or drapes
- Pests nesting inside personal items or boxes that you own
- Replacing personal belongings damaged by water, mold, or pests

### 3. Wasp nests on public vs. private property

If our inspectors find an active ground wasp nest, our action depends on where it is located:

- **Public property:** If the nest is on a city street, sidewalk, park, or public land, we will refer the hazard directly to the responsible sister agency for safe removal.
- **Private residential property:** HHVC will require the property owner or manager to hire a licensed Pest Control Operator (PCO) to eliminate the nest safely.

## Inspection thresholds you should know

To ensure our enforcement is fair and legal, HHVC follows strict standards under the San Francisco Health Code:

### 1. Visible mold threshold

- **SFDPH can take action only when active mold is growing on structural surfaces (walls or ceilings) and the affected area totals at least 10 square feet.**
- We do not inspect or enforce on minor surface mildew under 10 square feet, nor do we test or approve third-party indoor mold testing kits.

### 2. SRO and residential hotel shared spaces

- Common areas in SROs and residential hotels—including shared kitchens, communal bathrooms, hallways, garbage rooms, and courtyards—must be kept completely clean, sanitary, and free of vectors.
- Any tenant living in an SRO can report shared-space sanitation or pest issues directly to 311 for priority inspection.

---

## What to do before you report to the City

If you rent your home or apartment, you must take these steps before requesting a City inspection:

1. **Notify your landlord in writing:** Text, email, or write a letter to your property owner, manager, or building operator as soon as you spot a pest, mold, or sanitation problem.
2. **Allow 72 hours:** Give your landlord at least 72 hours to respond or start fixing the problem.
3. **Report to 311:** If the landlord does not respond, refuses to help, or the pest or mold problem continues after 72 hours, report the issue to 311 to request an HHVC inspection. _Note: You do not need to wait 72 hours for urgent, severe health and safety hazards._
